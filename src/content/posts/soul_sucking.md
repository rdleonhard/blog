An Amish guy sold me a donut at our local farmer's market, and of course I needed cash because he doesn't accept credit cards. Just now as I write this I wondered if I'd get into trouble for negatively characterizing him, but why would they be online? And reading some obscure tech blog at that?

And why did they turn away from the modern world all those years ago? I remember stories about tribal people not wanting their picture taken as it might steal their soul. It was a mildly amusing anecdote when I was younger. Not funny anymore.

Can AI read what I just wrote and infer a soul from it? Could a sufficiently powerful AI read all the data collected about me in some NSA data center in some inconspicuous area of Utah, then recreate my soul? Will I wonder around alternate histories of the US for the next thousand years?

That'd be pretty cool.

As a veteran, I sometimes wonder if the Arlington memorial could be updated to where you can visit grave sites and a simulation of the veteran would show up on screen and talk to you. The recreation would be developed by the veteran's own voice, if available, and any writings, social media posts, emails, anything else written while alive.

I'd do it. I'd also go there and commune with the ghosts of my fallen brethren. I mean, I do that anyway, but it'd be cool to talk with them.

I could implement this myself with an elizaOS character.json, a NVIDIA Jetson Nanao, speaker and mic modules, a screen, and data to train the AI on.

Who would oppose this idea? IDK, a lot of people are weird about this kind of thing. It worries them, like the soul of the fallen wouldn't make it to the other world or something. Or it violates standards of decency or something. But to me, it's like having your picture taken. It doesn't *actually* steal your soul. But maybe something similar.

I remember reading about Roman death masks that were kept at people's houses, basically these statues of your ancestors looking at you all day. Like judging you. You would definitely feel the weight on your shoulders, but I suspect that's a good thing. Like maybe men should grow up knowing how serious this all is.

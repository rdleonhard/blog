Perfect weather is overrated for walking. It attracts plans, expectations, and people filming the
proof that the day happened.

Give me the uncertain day instead: a low sky, a wind that has not decided what it means, the kind of
temperature that makes every block feel negotiable. Weather like that introduces noise into the
system. It makes the city harder to summarize and better to inhabit.

## Leaving the Room

Walking in imperfect weather changes the pace of attention. You notice which awnings work. You learn
where the pavement gathers water. You become grateful for a warm bakery window in a way that no
recommendation engine can pre-render.

Some days are not built for arrival. They are built for a longer route home, outside the algorithmic
room, under a sky with no login state.

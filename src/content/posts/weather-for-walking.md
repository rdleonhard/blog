Perfect weather is overrated for walking. It attracts plans, expectations, and people carrying
drinks with complicated lids.

Give me the uncertain day instead: a low sky, a wind that has not decided what it means, the kind of
temperature that makes every block feel negotiable. Weather like that keeps the city honest. It
removes the performance of leisure and leaves only the act of moving through a place.

## The Pace of It

Walking in imperfect weather changes the pace of attention. You notice which awnings work. You
learn where the pavement gathers water. You become grateful for a warm bakery window in a way that
would be impossible under a flawless blue sky.

Some days are not built for arrival. They are built for a longer route home.

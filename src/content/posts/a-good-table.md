The best table in a home is rarely the most expensive object in the room. It is the one that has
quietly learned how to be useful at several speeds.

In the morning, it holds a cup of coffee and the first unreasonable optimism of the day. By
afternoon, it has collected mail, keys, a notebook, and one object no one remembers placing there.
At night, it becomes a stage for food and conversation.

## Useful Surfaces

There is a temptation to keep a table perfect, as if perfection were hospitality. But the tables I
like best have a little evidence on them: a ring from a glass, a softened edge, a drawer that opens
with a small complaint.

A good table is not furniture pretending to be sculpture. It is an agreement to gather.

The best table in a home is rarely the most impressive object in the room. It is the one that has
quietly learned how to stay physical.

Everything else is getting stranger. The phone predicts the message. The laptop suggests the next
sentence. The speaker waits for a wake word like a polite ghost. By afternoon, the room is dense
with systems that do not look like furniture but still rearrange the day.

The table just holds things: coffee, mail, keys, a notebook, a receipt, the first unreasonable
optimism of the morning.

## Useful Resistance

There is comfort in an object that does not personalize itself. It does not learn your preferences,
surface recommendations, or ask whether you want to continue where you left off.

A good table is not anti-technology. It is a reminder that not every useful thing needs to become
smart.
